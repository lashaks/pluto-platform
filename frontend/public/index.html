FAQ

Common questions.

Is this real trading?

No. All trading takes place in a simulated (demo) environment using real market data. When you pass the evaluation and receive a funded account, profits are still generated in a simulated environment. Payouts are performance-based rewards paid from our operational revenue.

Are challenge fees refundable?

No. All challenge fees are non-refundable. The fee pays for access to the simulated evaluation environment.

What's the difference between 1-Step and 2-Step?

1-Step has a single phase with a 10% profit target and 8% max drawdown. 2-Step has two phases (8% then 5% target) with a higher 10% max drawdown. 2-Step is cheaper and more forgiving on drawdown. 1-Step is faster if you're confident.

What is the 30% consistency rule?

No single trading day can account for more than 30% of your total profit. This ensures you're a consistent trader, not a gambler who got lucky once.

What is static drawdown?

Your maximum loss floor is fixed at your starting balance minus the drawdown percentage. It never moves up. If you start with $100K and have 8% drawdown, your floor is $92K forever — even if you grow to $110K. This is more trader-friendly than trailing drawdown.

When do I need to complete KYC?

KYC is required before you receive your funded account, not before purchasing or trading a challenge. You can buy and trade immediately.

How do payouts work?

On funded accounts, you can request a payout anytime you have profit above $50 with no open positions. We process payouts via USDT, USDC, or bank transfer. You keep 80% of the profit.

Can I use Expert Advisors (EAs)?

Yes. EAs, scripts, and indicators are allowed on both cTrader and MT5. Copy trading from your own accounts is permitted. Copy trading from external signal services or shared accounts is prohibited.

What strategies are prohibited?

Arbitrage, latency exploitation, HFT, tick scalping, hedging across multiple accounts, trading during restricted news events, account sharing, and any form of market manipulation or simulated environment exploitation.

Can I hold trades over the weekend?

During evaluation, yes. On funded accounts, all positions must be closed before the market closes on Friday (3:45 PM EST). Positions left open will be auto-closed. This is a soft rule — no breach, but the position will be closed.

Is hedging allowed?

Hedging within a single account is allowed within your leverage and lot size limits. Hedging across multiple Pluto Capital accounts is strictly prohibited and will result in termination of all accounts.

Is there a minimum trade duration?

Your average trade duration must be at least 2 minutes. Individual trades can be shorter, but if your overall average drops below 2 minutes, you may be flagged for high-frequency or tick-scalping behavior.

What counts as "gambling behavior"?

Trading strategies that are "all or nothing" — such as risking the entire account on a single trade, extreme variation in lot sizes between trades, or patterns showing no consistent strategy — are prohibited. We're looking for disciplined, repeatable trading.
